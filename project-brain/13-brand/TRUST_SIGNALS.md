# Trust Signals

## Purpose

This document defines the trust signals Northdown Computers AI Systems & Automation should communicate publicly. It is guidance for future messaging, not final website copy.

## Existing business credibility

Northdown should present itself as an operating business that understands real client environments, not as a speculative AI experiment.

Signals to emphasise:

- Practical business support orientation.
- Focus on small and mid-sized business operations.
- Willingness to start with discovery and audit instead of forcing a tool.
- Clear boundaries around fit, scope, and risk.

Do not imply unsupported credentials, regulated compliance, guarantees, or case results without approved evidence.

## Operational process

Northdown builds trust by showing a clear process from enquiry to delivery and improvement.

Signals to emphasise:

- First contact.
- Discovery.
- Audit.
- Proposal.
- Delivery.
- Validation.
- Handoff.
- Optimisation or retainer.

This process shows that AI and automation work are handled as operational change, not casual experimentation.

## Documentation discipline

Northdown should communicate that serious AI and automation work requires written context and source truth.

Signals to emphasise:

- Project-brain before implementation.
- Source-map notes for extracted knowledge.
- Decision records for important choices.
- Handoff notes for continuity.
- Clear out-of-scope documentation.

Documentation should be framed as practical operating discipline, not paperwork for its own sake.

## Validation discipline

Northdown should show that work is checked before it is treated as complete.

Signals to emphasise:

- Acceptance criteria.
- Representative examples.
- Read-back of documentation.
- Risk and access checks.
- Known issues and follow-up lists.
- Exact validation reporting.

Do not claim a system works without explaining how it will be validated.

## Human oversight

Northdown should make clear that people remain responsible for judgment-heavy, sensitive, or business-critical decisions.

Signals to emphasise:

- Human approval points.
- Exception handling.
- Least-privilege access.
- Clear ownership.
- Escalation and incident response.
- Automation that supports staff instead of replacing accountability.

## Trust guardrails

- Do not use fear-based AI messaging.
- Do not overstate maturity before evidence exists.
- Do not claim compliance, security certification, or regulated expertise without approval.
- Do not present planning docs as proof of delivered client outcomes.
- Keep public claims tied to documented process and approved source truth.
