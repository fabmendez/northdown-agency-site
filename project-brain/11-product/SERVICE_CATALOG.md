# Service Catalog

## Purpose

This catalog defines what Northdown Computers AI Systems & Automation sells at a service level. It does not define pricing, stack choices, implementation details, or platform commitments.

## Positioning

Northdown sells practical AI systems, automation workflows, internal tools, knowledge systems, agentic engineering, and operational AI infrastructure for small and mid-sized businesses.

The work starts from operational problems, not from tools. Discovery, audit, validation, handoff, and governance are part of the offer.

## Offerings

## AI Systems Audits

A structured review of a business workflow, information flow, tool usage, data sensitivity, and automation readiness.

Best for:

- Businesses considering AI or automation but unsure where to start.
- Teams with repeated manual work across documents, email, spreadsheets, or shared systems.
- Operators who need risk, access, and human approval boundaries before building.

Typical deliverables:

- Discovery summary.
- Current workflow map.
- Automation opportunity list.
- Risk and access notes.
- Recommended first project or no-fit recommendation.

## Automation Workflows

Design and delivery of a controlled workflow that reduces repeated manual work while preserving human approval where needed.

Best for:

- Repeated admin tasks.
- Intake, follow-up, reporting, document preparation, or handoff processes.
- Workflows with clear inputs, outputs, owners, and review points.

Typical deliverables:

- Target workflow map.
- Automation design.
- Human approval points.
- Validation checklist.
- Handoff notes.

## Internal Tools

Business-specific tools that help staff manage operational work more clearly than disconnected spreadsheets, inboxes, and manual trackers.

Best for:

- Growing teams outgrowing informal processes.
- Operational dashboards, task views, internal request flows, or lightweight process tools.
- Teams that need visibility and repeatability without unnecessary complexity.

Typical deliverables:

- Tool scope and user roles.
- Workflow and data handling notes.
- Approved build task when implementation is authorised.
- User guide and support notes.
- Validation report.

## Knowledge Systems

Structured knowledge bases and retrieval workflows that make business information easier to find, maintain, and use responsibly.

Best for:

- Businesses with useful information spread across documents, folders, notes, inboxes, or staff memory.
- Teams that need repeatable answers, onboarding support, or internal process guidance.
- Work where source attribution and context hygiene matter.

Typical deliverables:

- Knowledge source inventory.
- Source-map or knowledge structure.
- Governance and attribution rules.
- Review workflow for stale or sensitive information.
- Handoff guide.

## Agentic Engineering

Design of AI-assisted operating systems where agents, tools, instructions, source truth, validation, and human approval work together.

Best for:

- Repeatable AI-assisted work that needs traceability.
- Internal delivery systems with clear source truth and guardrails.
- Teams that need agents to support work without uncontrolled autonomy.

Typical deliverables:

- Agent operating contract.
- Project-brain or source-truth structure.
- Tool and approval boundaries.
- Validation and handoff procedures.
- Risk and context hygiene controls.

## Operational AI Infrastructure

Planning and delivery support for the operational layer around AI use: governance, access, validation, knowledge, workflows, handoff, and improvement cycles.

Best for:

- Businesses moving from AI experiments into repeatable operations.
- Teams needing controlled adoption, auditability, and ongoing improvement.
- Work that requires human review, documentation, and resilience planning.

Typical deliverables:

- Operating model.
- Governance policies.
- Validation approach.
- Risk register and incident response notes.
- Retention or optimisation plan.

## Catalog boundaries

- No pricing is defined here.
- No stack, platform, vendor, or dependency is selected here.
- No service guarantees, compliance claims, or regulated advice are implied.
- Implementation requires an approved task, scope, validation plan, and decision records where relevant.
