# Deliverable Definitions

## Purpose

This document defines the expected input, output, success criteria, and validation for each Northdown Computers AI Systems & Automation service. It is service-level planning, not implementation code.

## AI Systems Audits

### Input

- Business context and operating goals.
- Current workflow notes, examples, and pain points.
- Known systems, documents, spreadsheets, inboxes, or tools where approved.
- Sensitive data, access, and human approval constraints.

### Output

- Discovery summary.
- Current workflow map.
- Automation opportunity list.
- Risk and access notes.
- Recommended first project, defer recommendation, or no-fit recommendation.

### Success criteria

- The client understands where AI or automation can help and where it should not be used yet.
- The recommended next step is practical, scoped, and tied to a business workflow.
- Risks, access needs, and human approval points are documented.

### Validation

- Read back audit findings against discovery notes.
- Confirm open questions and assumptions are visible.
- Confirm no pricing, stack, or implementation commitment is made without approval.

## Automation Workflows

### Input

- Approved workflow scope.
- Defined inputs, outputs, owners, and exceptions.
- Acceptance criteria and human review points.
- Data access and operational constraints.

### Output

- Target workflow map.
- Automation design.
- Handoff and exception handling notes.
- Validation checklist.
- Implementation task when separately approved.

### Success criteria

- The workflow reduces repeated manual work without removing required human judgment.
- Owners, triggers, outputs, and failure paths are clear.
- The client can validate the workflow against realistic examples.

### Validation

- Check workflow against acceptance criteria.
- Test or review representative examples when implementation exists.
- Confirm human approval points and access limits are documented.

## Internal Tools

### Input

- Approved tool scope and user roles.
- Target workflows and data needs.
- Current process examples.
- Acceptance criteria, constraints, and support expectations.

### Output

- Tool definition and user workflow.
- Role and data handling notes.
- Approved implementation task when build work is authorised.
- User guide, support notes, and validation report.

### Success criteria

- The tool supports a real operational workflow.
- Users understand what the tool does and does not do.
- The tool improves visibility, consistency, or handoff quality.

### Validation

- Validate against role-based workflows and acceptance criteria.
- Confirm no unapproved stack, dependency, auth, data, or deployment choice is introduced.
- Confirm handoff documentation is usable by the intended users.

## Knowledge Systems

### Input

- Approved knowledge sources.
- Source ownership and sensitivity notes.
- Intended user questions or use cases.
- Attribution, update, and review requirements.

### Output

- Knowledge source inventory.
- Source-map structure or knowledge organisation plan.
- Attribution and context hygiene rules.
- Review workflow for stale, sensitive, or unclear material.

### Success criteria

- Useful information is easier to find and cite.
- Knowledge notes distinguish source truth from assumptions.
- Sensitive or stale material has a review path.

### Validation

- Confirm each note cites its source where relevant.
- Confirm extracted knowledge is concise and tied to a project need.
- Confirm no reference repo or source material was modified without approval.

## Agentic Engineering

### Input

- Defined business workflow or agent-supported process.
- Source truth, task rules, tool boundaries, and human approval requirements.
- Risk, validation, and handoff expectations.

### Output

- Agent operating contract.
- Project-brain or source-truth structure.
- Tool and permission boundaries.
- Validation, incident, and handoff procedures.
- Decision records where architecture or governance changes are approved.

### Success criteria

- Agents have clear scope, source truth, and stop conditions.
- Human approval is preserved for judgment-heavy or high-risk work.
- Work is traceable through tasks, decisions, validation, and handoffs.

### Validation

- Review against governance, quality, and operations policies.
- Confirm source attribution is present when source repos informed decisions.
- Confirm no unapproved autonomy, tool access, dependency, or infrastructure decision is introduced.

## Operational AI Infrastructure

### Input

- Business operating goals.
- Existing process and governance context.
- Risk, access, backup, validation, and support expectations.
- Approved scope for operational AI adoption.

### Output

- Operating model.
- Governance and quality policies.
- Validation and incident response approach.
- Risk register.
- Handoff and optimisation plan.

### Success criteria

- AI-assisted work has operational controls, not only prompts or tools.
- Risks, validation, source truth, and handoff are documented.
- Future implementation can proceed from approved tasks and decisions.

### Validation

- Check outputs against project-brain requirements and policies.
- Confirm operational controls are documented before implementation.
- Confirm no pricing, stack, compliance, or infrastructure claim is implied without approval.
