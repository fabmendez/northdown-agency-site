# Conversion Paths

## Purpose

This document maps how visitors should move from initial awareness to client intake. It does not define implementation, analytics, forms, or automation.

## Core path

```text
Visitor
  ↓
Interest
  ↓
Discovery
  ↓
Qualification
  ↓
Contact
  ↓
Client intake
```

## Stage 1: Visitor

### Visitor state

The person has arrived with a vague or specific interest in AI, automation, internal tools, or operational improvement.

### Website job

- Establish who Northdown serves.
- State what Northdown does in practical terms.
- Avoid hype and unclear claims.
- Offer clear paths to services, process, resources, or contact.

### Key pages

- Home.
- Resources.
- About.

## Stage 2: Interest

### Visitor state

The visitor recognises a relevant problem: repeated admin, disconnected tools, unclear workflows, or pressure to adopt AI sensibly.

### Website job

- Show operational problems Northdown can help with.
- Present the service catalog.
- Explain good-fit and poor-fit boundaries.
- Build trust through process and validation discipline.

### Key pages

- Services.
- Process.
- About.
- Resources.

## Stage 3: Discovery

### Visitor state

The visitor is considering whether to start a conversation.

### Website job

- Explain discovery before solution design.
- Set expectations for current workflow, pain points, systems, owners, inputs, outputs, approvals, constraints, and success definition.
- Make it clear that pricing, stack, or implementation commitments require scope first.

### Key pages

- Process.
- Services.
- Contact.

## Stage 4: Qualification

### Visitor state

The visitor is deciding whether their request is a good fit.

### Website job

- Communicate good-fit and poor-fit criteria.
- Surface red flags gently through boundaries and expectations.
- Encourage practical, scoped enquiries.

### Key pages

- Services.
- Process.
- Contact.

## Stage 5: Contact

### Visitor state

The visitor is ready to make contact.

### Website job

- Ask for enough context to route the enquiry.
- Avoid requesting sensitive data upfront.
- Explain what happens next.
- Link to privacy guidance.

### Key pages

- Contact.
- Privacy.

## Stage 6: Client intake

### Visitor state

The visitor becomes a prospective client record or structured enquiry.

### Website job

- Support the intake fields in `project-brain/12-sales/CLIENT_INTAKE_TEMPLATE.md`.
- Route the enquiry toward discovery, audit, proposal, deferral, or no-fit decision.
- Preserve human review for qualification.

### Key docs

- `project-brain/12-sales/CLIENT_INTAKE_TEMPLATE.md`.
- `project-brain/12-sales/QUALIFICATION_FRAMEWORK.md`.
- `project-brain/12-sales/DISCOVERY_QUESTIONNAIRE.md`.

## Conversion guardrails

- Do not pressure visitors with exaggerated AI claims.
- Do not promise outcomes before discovery.
- Do not collect sensitive information without a clear reason and privacy guidance.
- Do not imply pricing, timeline, stack, or compliance commitments.
- Keep the path practical: understand the workflow, qualify the fit, then scope the next step.
