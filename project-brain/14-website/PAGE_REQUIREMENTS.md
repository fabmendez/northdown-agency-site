# Page Requirements

## Purpose

This document defines page-level goals, audiences, trust signals, primary calls to action, and required sections. It is not final website copy.

## Home

### Goal

Explain the business clearly and move relevant visitors toward discovery.

### Audience

Business owners, managers, and operators dealing with repeated admin, disconnected tools, unclear workflows, or practical AI adoption questions.

### Trust signals

- Existing business credibility.
- Practical operational process.
- Documentation discipline.
- Validation discipline.
- Human oversight.

### Primary CTA

Start a discovery conversation.

### Required sections

- First-viewport business identity and offer summary.
- Operational problems Northdown helps with.
- Service overview.
- Process summary.
- Trust and safety principles.
- Contact path.

## Services

### Goal

Explain what Northdown sells and help visitors identify the right service path.

### Audience

Visitors comparing possible help with audits, automation, internal tools, knowledge systems, agentic engineering, or operational AI infrastructure.

### Trust signals

- Clear service boundaries.
- No tool-first claims.
- Validation and handoff included.
- Human approval where needed.

### Primary CTA

Request an operational review.

### Required sections

- Service catalog overview.
- AI Systems Audits.
- Automation Workflows.
- Internal Tools.
- Knowledge Systems.
- Agentic Engineering.
- Operational AI Infrastructure.
- Good-fit / poor-fit guidance.
- Link to process and contact.

## About

### Goal

Build confidence in Northdown's operating approach and practical positioning.

### Audience

Visitors who need trust before starting a conversation.

### Trust signals

- Existing business credibility.
- Operational discipline.
- Clear boundaries.
- Human oversight.
- Documentation and validation culture.

### Primary CTA

See the process or contact Northdown.

### Required sections

- Business orientation.
- Who Northdown serves.
- Working principles.
- Trust signals.
- Boundaries and responsible AI stance.
- Contact path.

## Process

### Goal

Show how a client moves from first contact to discovery, qualification, delivery, validation, handoff, and optimisation.

### Audience

Potential clients who want to understand how work is controlled before committing.

### Trust signals

- Discovery before solution design.
- Clear qualification.
- Risk and access review.
- Validation before handoff.
- Ongoing optimisation only when useful.

### Primary CTA

Start with discovery.

### Required sections

- First contact.
- Discovery.
- Audit.
- Proposal.
- Delivery.
- Validation.
- Handoff.
- Optimisation / retainer.
- What clients need to provide.

## Contact

### Goal

Capture enough information to qualify the enquiry and route it to discovery or deferral.

### Audience

Visitors ready to explain a workflow problem or request help.

### Trust signals

- Clear intake expectations.
- No sensitive data requested upfront.
- Human review.
- Plain next steps.

### Primary CTA

Submit an enquiry or request discovery.

### Required sections

- Contact method.
- Intake guidance.
- What to include.
- What not to send yet.
- Expected next step.
- Privacy link.

## Resources

### Goal

Support education and trust with practical explanations and future source-led material.

### Audience

Visitors researching AI systems, automation, internal tools, knowledge systems, and governance.

### Trust signals

- Practical guidance.
- Clear source attribution when relevant.
- No hype.
- Governance and validation focus.

### Primary CTA

Move from learning to discovery.

### Required sections

- Resource categories.
- FAQs when approved.
- Guides or notes when available.
- Link back to services and process.
- Contact path.

## Privacy

### Goal

Explain how contact and enquiry information should be handled at a high level.

### Audience

Visitors considering contact, especially where sensitive business information may be involved.

### Trust signals

- Clear boundaries around sensitive information.
- Human review.
- Least-privilege mindset.
- No unnecessary data requests.

### Primary CTA

Return to contact or services.

### Required sections

- Information visitors may provide.
- Sensitive information guidance.
- Contact-form handling expectations.
- Third-party/tooling note when approved.
- Contact route for privacy questions.
