# Qualification Framework

## Purpose

This framework helps Northdown Computers AI Systems & Automation decide whether a client request is a good fit, poor fit, red flag, or not ready yet.

## Good fit

A project is a good fit when:

- There is a clear operational workflow to improve.
- The client can describe the current process and pain points.
- The workflow has repeated manual effort.
- Inputs, outputs, owners, and approval points can be identified.
- The client accepts discovery before solution design.
- The first increment can be scoped small enough to validate.
- Sensitive data and access constraints can be handled clearly.
- Success can be defined in practical business terms.
- The client values documentation, validation, handoff, and controlled adoption.

## Poor fit

A project is a poor fit when:

- The request is vague AI experimentation without a business workflow.
- The client wants technology before discovery.
- The client expects AI to replace judgment, accountability, or staff oversight.
- The scope is too broad to review safely.
- Success cannot be validated.
- The work requires regulated advice outside approved capability.
- Required access, data sensitivity, or ownership is unclear.
- The client does not want documentation, validation, or handoff.

## Red flags

Stop and clarify before proceeding when:

- The client asks for pricing before scope is understood.
- The client wants unapproved stack, platform, integration, deployment, or dependency commitments.
- The client wants sensitive data handled without governance.
- The client asks to bypass human approval for judgment-heavy decisions.
- The workflow owner is unclear or unavailable.
- Required source material cannot be provided or validated.
- The client expects guaranteed outcomes before audit or discovery.
- The project depends on legal, medical, financial, security compliance, or regulated claims not separately approved.
- The requested work would require repo extraction, reference repo modification, network access, installs, or builds without approval.

## Project readiness

### Low readiness

Indicators:

- Problem is vague.
- Workflow is not mapped.
- Owners are unclear.
- Inputs and outputs are unknown.
- Access and sensitivity are unclear.

Recommended action:

- Do not propose implementation.
- Recommend discovery or operational audit.

### Medium readiness

Indicators:

- Workflow is mostly understood.
- Pain points are clear.
- Some inputs, outputs, owners, or constraints need clarification.
- Success criteria need refinement.

Recommended action:

- Run focused discovery or audit.
- Prepare a scoped proposal only after open questions are answered.

### High readiness

Indicators:

- Workflow, owners, inputs, outputs, constraints, and approval points are clear.
- Success criteria are practical and testable.
- Client can provide examples and validate outcomes.
- Scope can be kept small.

Recommended action:

- Prepare a proposal with problem, current state, proposed approach, deliverables, validation, risks, and out of scope.

## Qualification outcome

Use one of these outcomes:

- Qualified for discovery.
- Qualified for audit.
- Qualified for scoped proposal.
- Deferred pending information.
- Poor fit.
- Stop: risk or approval needed.
