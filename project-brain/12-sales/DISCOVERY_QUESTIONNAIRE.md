# Discovery Questionnaire

## Purpose

This questionnaire helps Northdown Computers AI Systems & Automation understand the client's current workflow, operational pain, readiness, and constraints before proposing a solution.

## Business context

- Business name:
- Contact name and role:
- Team or department affected:
- Brief description of the business:
- What prompted the enquiry?
- What would make this work valuable to the business?

## Current workflow

- What workflow or process should be reviewed?
- What starts the workflow?
- What happens step by step today?
- Who performs each step?
- Where does the workflow slow down, fail, or depend on memory?
- What happens when the normal process has an exception?
- What parts already work well and should not be disrupted?

## Pain points

- What is frustrating, slow, duplicated, or unclear?
- What errors or delays happen repeatedly?
- Which tasks feel like avoidable manual work?
- Where do handoffs break down?
- What information is hard to find?
- What is the operational cost of leaving the process as it is?

## Systems involved

- Which tools, documents, spreadsheets, inboxes, folders, or systems are involved?
- Where is the source information stored?
- Which systems are trusted and which are messy or incomplete?
- Are there templates, examples, reports, or standard operating procedures?
- Are any systems sensitive, restricted, or client-facing?

## Manual effort

- How often does this workflow happen?
- How many people are involved?
- How much time is spent on the manual parts?
- Which steps are repeated most often?
- Which steps require judgment rather than simple processing?
- What workarounds are currently used?

## Owners

- Who owns the workflow?
- Who performs the day-to-day work?
- Who approves decisions or exceptions?
- Who will validate whether a change works?
- Who needs to be trained or handed over to?

## Inputs

- What information is needed to begin the workflow?
- Where do inputs come from?
- What format are inputs in?
- What inputs are often missing, late, duplicated, or incorrect?
- Which inputs are sensitive or restricted?

## Outputs

- What should the workflow produce?
- Who uses the output?
- What format should the output be in?
- What makes an output correct, useful, or ready?
- What failure or rework happens when outputs are wrong?

## Human approval requirements

- Which decisions must remain with a person?
- Which outputs need review before use?
- Who approves exceptions?
- What should automation never decide alone?
- What audit trail or handoff notes are needed?

## Constraints

- What must not change?
- Are there deadlines, access limits, or operational restrictions?
- Are there legal, compliance, security, or client confidentiality concerns?
- Are there systems that cannot be connected or modified?
- Are there budget, timing, staffing, or support constraints? Do not capture pricing assumptions here.

## Success definition

- What would make this project successful?
- What would count as a failed outcome?
- What should be faster, clearer, safer, or easier?
- What evidence would show the workflow improved?
- What should be validated before handoff?
