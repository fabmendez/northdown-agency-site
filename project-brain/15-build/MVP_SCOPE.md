# MVP Scope

## Purpose

This document defines a build-ready MVP scope without choosing a stack or writing implementation code.

## Must have

The MVP must include:

- Home page structure based on `project-brain/14-website/PAGE_REQUIREMENTS.md`.
- Services page covering all six approved service areas.
- About page focused on business orientation, trust, and boundaries.
- Process page covering first contact through optimisation.
- Contact page with approved contact/intake path once the contact-flow decision is made.
- Privacy page with approved privacy content before publication.
- Navigation linking the primary pages.
- Trust signals: operational process, documentation discipline, validation discipline, human oversight.
- No pricing, stack claims, unsupported case studies, compliance claims, or service guarantees.

## Should have

The MVP should include if content and decisions are approved:

- Resources landing page shell.
- FAQs from approved source docs.
- Process diagram or simple visual explanation.
- Service summary cards or structured service sections.
- Good-fit and poor-fit guidance.
- Clear contact expectations and sensitive information warning.
- Basic SEO metadata once SEO requirements are approved.

## Could have

The MVP could include later:

- Individual service pages.
- Case studies, if approved evidence exists.
- Resource articles or guides.
- Downloadable discovery checklist.
- Booking integration.
- Analytics dashboard or event tracking.
- Client portal or operating base entry point.

## Not now

Do not include in the MVP:

- Pricing pages or pricing claims.
- Client case studies without approved evidence.
- Testimonials without approved evidence.
- Auth, accounts, portal, or protected routes.
- CMS unless a decision record approves it.
- Analytics unless a decision record approves it.
- Complex animation or asset-heavy design before performance expectations are approved.
- Any dependency, integration, hosting, form provider, or stack choice by assumption.

## MVP readiness checklist

Before MVP implementation starts, confirm:

- Stack decision approved.
- Hosting decision approved.
- Contact flow decision approved.
- Privacy content approved.
- Initial page copy approved.
- Brand assets approved or placeholder policy approved.
- Validation commands known for the chosen stack.
