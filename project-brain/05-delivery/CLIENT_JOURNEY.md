# Client Journey

## Purpose

This document maps how a client moves from first contact to ongoing optimisation with Northdown Computers AI Systems & Automation. It keeps the journey operational, controlled, and suitable for small and mid-sized businesses.

## 1. First contact

### Client intent

The client has a workflow problem, an automation idea, or pressure to adopt AI safely.

### Humans do

- Listen for the business problem before discussing tools.
- Confirm the business type, operational pain, and urgency.
- Set expectations that discovery comes before solution design.
- Avoid pricing, platform, timeline, or outcome promises before scope is understood.

### Agents do

- Help structure enquiry notes.
- Extract likely workflow areas, stakeholders, risks, and follow-up questions.
- Prepare a first-contact summary for human review.

### Automation happens

- Intake notes can be standardised.
- Follow-up tasks can be created after human approval.
- Basic information requests can be drafted.

### Deliverables

- Enquiry summary.
- Initial fit notes.
- Discovery call agenda.

## 2. Discovery

### Client intent

The client explains how work currently happens and what is causing friction.

### Humans do

- Interview decision-makers and day-to-day operators.
- Identify the real workflow, not only the requested tool.
- Confirm sensitive data, access constraints, and human approval needs.
- Decide whether the opportunity is a good fit.

### Agents do

- Convert notes into structured process observations.
- Identify repeated tasks, handoffs, decision points, and missing information.
- Draft clarification questions for human review.

### Automation happens

- Discovery notes can be organised into a consistent format.
- Repeated issues can be grouped by workflow area.
- Follow-up questions can be prepared from gaps in the notes.

### Deliverables

- Discovery summary.
- Stakeholder and workflow map.
- Known constraints and open questions.

## 3. Audit

### Client intent

The client needs a clear view of what can be improved and what should not be automated yet.

### Humans do

- Review workflows, tools, documents, and risks.
- Separate high-value opportunities from low-value automation ideas.
- Judge whether a workflow is stable enough to automate.
- Confirm human approval points for judgment-heavy work.

### Agents do

- Help compare current-state notes against the service ladder.
- Draft opportunity lists, risk notes, and candidate workflow maps.
- Highlight missing source material or unclear ownership.

### Automation happens

- Workflow observations can be grouped into audit categories.
- Candidate opportunities can be ranked using approved criteria.
- Audit findings can be drafted into a standard document.

### Deliverables

- Operational audit summary.
- Automation opportunity list.
- Risk and access notes.
- Recommended first workflow or no-fit recommendation.

## 4. Proposal

### Client intent

The client decides whether to proceed with a defined first project.

### Humans do

- Define scope, acceptance criteria, constraints, and responsibilities.
- Explain what is included and what is out of scope.
- Confirm data access, approvals, validation, and handoff expectations.
- Keep the proposal tied to one practical operational outcome.

### Agents do

- Draft scope options from approved audit material.
- Convert findings into proposal language.
- Check for unsupported claims, missing acceptance criteria, or unclear assumptions.

### Automation happens

- Proposal sections can be assembled from approved discovery and audit notes.
- Risk and constraint checklists can be reused.
- Handoff and validation requirements can be inserted consistently.

### Deliverables

- Proposal document.
- Scope and exclusions.
- Acceptance criteria.
- Delivery plan.
- Client responsibilities.

## 5. Delivery

### Client intent

The client receives the agreed workflow, internal tool, AI system, or operational process improvement.

### Humans do

- Lead client communication and decisions.
- Approve designs, workflow changes, access, and release readiness.
- Build or supervise implementation once a future technical task is approved.
- Keep work inside the agreed scope.

### Agents do

- Support planning, documentation, checklists, test cases, and review notes.
- Help maintain project-brain context and task handoffs.
- Flag contradictions, missing approvals, or scope drift.

### Automation happens

- Repeated project documentation can be templated.
- Task status, validation notes, and handoff drafts can be standardised.
- Internal checks can help catch gaps before human review.

### Deliverables

- Approved workflow design.
- Delivery checklist.
- Working system or process output when an implementation task exists.
- Draft operating documentation.

## 6. Validation

### Client intent

The client confirms the delivered work behaves as agreed and is safe to use.

### Humans do

- Test against acceptance criteria.
- Review edge cases and failure paths.
- Confirm access, security, and human approval points.
- Decide whether the work is ready for handoff.

### Agents do

- Generate validation checklists from requirements.
- Compare test results against acceptance criteria.
- Summarise failures and unresolved risks.

### Automation happens

- Repeatable checks can be run where approved.
- Validation reports can be assembled from test results and review notes.
- Known gaps can be tracked into follow-up tasks.

### Deliverables

- Validation report.
- Acceptance checklist.
- Known issues and follow-up list.
- Go/no-go recommendation for human decision.

## 7. Handoff

### Client intent

The client knows how to use, review, and maintain the delivered system or workflow.

### Humans do

- Walk through the finished workflow with the client.
- Explain responsibilities, limitations, escalation paths, and maintenance needs.
- Confirm documentation is understandable to the actual users.

### Agents do

- Draft user guides, admin notes, and internal handoff summaries.
- Create concise operating notes from approved delivery material.
- Prepare future improvement backlog items.

### Automation happens

- Handoff documents can be generated from approved project notes.
- Training checklists and support questions can be standardised.
- Future review reminders can be prepared if approved.

### Deliverables

- Client handoff guide.
- Internal handoff note.
- Support and escalation notes.
- Improvement backlog.

## 8. Optimisation / retainer

### Client intent

The client keeps improving the system after real-world use.

### Humans do

- Review performance, user feedback, and changing business needs.
- Prioritise improvements.
- Approve new work before implementation.
- Keep access and security boundaries current.

### Agents do

- Summarise feedback and operational metrics.
- Draft improvement options and maintenance notes.
- Help identify repeated issues or new automation candidates.

### Automation happens

- Review reminders can be scheduled where approved.
- Feedback can be grouped into themes.
- Improvement backlog updates can be standardised.

### Deliverables

- Review summary.
- Improvement backlog.
- Maintenance notes.
- Next approved task or retainer plan.
