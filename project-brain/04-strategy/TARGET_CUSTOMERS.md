# Target Customers

## Primary customers

Small and mid-sized businesses that have enough repeated operational work to benefit from automation, but not enough internal engineering capacity to design and maintain AI-enabled systems alone.

## Good-fit customer profiles

### Service businesses

Businesses that handle repeat enquiries, scheduling, reporting, quoting, follow-up, document preparation, or client communication.

Likely needs:

- Intake and follow-up workflows.
- Client document preparation.
- Internal knowledge search.
- Reporting and task tracking.
- Repeatable admin processes.

### Local and regional operators

Businesses with established day-to-day operations that rely on spreadsheets, email, shared folders, and manual coordination.

Likely needs:

- Process mapping.
- Spreadsheet-to-system transitions.
- Internal dashboards.
- Automated reminders and handoffs.
- Better information flow between teams.

### Growing teams

Businesses where work is becoming harder to manage through informal processes.

Likely needs:

- Operating procedures turned into usable tools.
- Lightweight internal systems.
- Role-based workflows.
- Repeatable client delivery processes.
- Management visibility without adding admin burden.

## Buying triggers

- Staff are repeating the same admin work every week.
- Important work is tracked across too many spreadsheets or inboxes.
- The business wants to use AI but needs a controlled, practical starting point.
- Existing tools are not joined up.
- Managers cannot easily see what is happening across operations.
- A team member is acting as the unofficial system that holds everything together.

## Poor-fit customers

- Businesses looking for vague AI experimentation without an operational problem.
- Buyers expecting instant replacement of staff or judgment-heavy work.
- Projects that require unclear access to sensitive data without governance.
- Large enterprise procurement projects outside the approved scope.
- Work requiring specialist legal, medical, financial, or regulated compliance advice unless separately approved.
